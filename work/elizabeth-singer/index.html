Assignment 1: Proposal Phase: Explore

My overall ideas all fit under the theme of a smart house. My interests in the smart home really center around leveraging technology for the disability community and privacy and security of smart home IOT devices. This led me to came up with the following 3 ideas for my capstone:

  1. Having a disability is the only marginalized group that almost everyone will be a part of in their lifetime. It doesn’t really make sense to me that we don’t innovate for those with disabilities more often given that we will almost all be impacted by accessibility (or lack of) efforts in our life. I think that taking care of my grandparents (who have problems with mobility and memory) has led me to be really interested in thinking about aging and disability and usability of technology for aging users.

    2. => This leads to the more specific idea of designing for older adults, specifically, my grandparents. They both have different needs: My grandfather has dementia and low vision, and my grandmother has limited mobility, though they navigate the same space. I have a lot of thoughts about the use of smart home IOT devices and questions about older adults and privacy. I also am inspired by the idea of going against the mass-produced one-size fits all approach for products for people with disabilities and am interested in the idea of the opposite, designing for one home, one situation.

  2. I’m also really interested in privacy, especially in regards to tech in the home: including privacy and security challenges for kids using video games and remote learning on school post COVID-19 pandemic (and intentionally designing for children privacy and security), elderly people’ use of voice activated technology, children’s privacy literacy with respect to smart home technology.

  3. I’m also interested in how long and short distance families relationships are impacted by the use of smart home iot devices.

    4. => I think the more specific idea here starts with a literature review of research already done, and then creating a computational design project that addresses needs or gaps

These ideas engage meaningfully with computation because it both involves critiquing the use of certain technology in this home setting (privacy), and also creating something likely involving significant coding.
