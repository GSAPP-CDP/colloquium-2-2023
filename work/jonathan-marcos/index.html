Proposal I: Easing Library Workloads and Access Throughout NYC

Libraries are more than just circulators of books- rather, they create concrete foundations for other services, such as passports, printing, taxes and community services. However, ever since the beginning of the age of the e-book, libraries have had these latter services heightened in usage, resulting in a degree of work overload from these libraries. On top of that, some populations throughout NYC may not be able to access these resources as easily due to certain factors such as language barriers and proximity. How do we use computation in order to figure out certain aiding factors (i.e. languages throughout NYC, income, proximity to libraries, library traffic, etc.) and from there, create expanded networks that ease the pressure of library traffic in the city?

Proposal II: Multi-Dimensional Traffic Tools

Many incumbent navigation tools utilize many factors in order to give the best result to users, such as real-time traffic, accident advisories, speed limits, etc. However, many navigation tools struggle to implement real-time weather in its algorithms. Weather plays a significant role in transportation- abrasive weather conditions can really reduce the comfort of the driver, make driving conditions worse and can alter the time it takes from going from Point A to Point B. How can we create traffic tools that account for real-time weather and give optimized results for users? In the urban scale, how can we use weather as another parameter in order to choose the best transportation method between walking, cycling, driving and the subway?

Proposal III: Revamping NYC's Funerary Infrastructure

As the world becomes more populous, the demand for burial plots in NYC will stay constant, but the supply for burial spaces will decrease over time. Eventually, burial plots will be very scarce and alternative funerary traditions may need to occur in order to prevent cemetery overcrowding. Cemeteries and funerary homes could potentially create a network that allows for the management of funerary space and optimize remaining spaces within these cemeteries. With computation, there is the possibility of making spatial tools that optimize the amount of space available left in cemeteries in order to respectfully put the most amount of individuals in, but this is only for the short-term as space will inevitably run out.

Easing the back-end functions of libraries

What is the current condition:

Libraries have more functions to them besides storing books, they are a database circulating vital information, such as passports, municipal forms and legal documents. Since the rise of e-books and especially the pandemic, these back-end functions have risen more into prominence, potentially overwhelming libraries with this surge of traffic.

What is the desired change and why:

How do we track the traffic of library functions (i.e. amount of passports being processed, amount of legal documents being printed, etc.) and use those data sets in order to create a network that optimizes and distributes these functions throughout NYC's existing library infrastructure?

What has already been done around this idea?

Some library functions (i.e. passport photos) require reservations in advance in order to have a set amount of individuals use these services a day, rather than just having walk-ins.

These reservations can be made online, and there will be indicators on whether services are available or not, and when reservations are available throughout the calendar.

GIS, Grasshopper, three.js, d3.js for statistics graphics, etc.

Who is this for?

Primarily libraries in order to create a distribution system and workload relief for their back-end products

Rental price prediction models for students and young professionals

What is the current condition:

Finding affordable rent in NYC can be exceptionally difficult, with expensive rates, shaky rental groups and requirements (i.e. have to be female, make 40x income, a credit score >680, etc.) that can easily overwhelm someone starting out their career in the city. With inflation, post-pandemic prices and a more-demanding than ever housing market, the supply for affordable and livable housing will only become more difficult to access.

What is the desired change and why:

How can we create an interface that secures students with various landlords that would have space for affordable housing near campuses and/or workplaces? How can we create a network that predicts which neighborhoods would be best for affordable housing and those that may be going through price-altering situations, such as gentrification and other developments?

What has already been done around this idea?

- NYC Affordable housing lottery- gives individuals the chance to sign up for new and fairly-priced units, except it typically takes over a year to get their residence of choice

- Universities offer housing, but of course, those get filled up fairly quickly

- University social media groups giving the opportunity for housing, but those can easily be hubs for scammers and other sketchy housing deals

- EHS in NYC, except their stuff is exceptionally expensive for an average student

GIS, Grasshopper, three.js, d3.js for statistics graphics, etc.
