1 – Idea1

Exploration of Diversity Generation of NYC Pocket Park Schemes Based on Machine Learning

NYC pocket parks

This project signifies a transformative approach to urban rejuvenation in NYC. As parking buildings become more prevalent, redesigning ground-level garages into pocket parks can enhance urban livability, providing green spaces and fostering community engagement in previously areas. Through ML workflow, designers are able to finish some repetitive tasks and come out some ideas more rapidly.

The workflow flow of the idea

1.Data Collection: First, a large amount of design data for NYC pocket parks, including plans and photos, needs to be collected. 2.Data Preprocessing: Convert this data into a format that can be processed by Machine learning model like GAN, such as resizing images to a consistent size. 3.Training the Model: Use the collected data to train the ML model until the generator can produce high-quality pocket park designs. 4.Generating Design Proposals: Use the trained GAN to generate a multitude of design proposals by changing the input. 5.Evaluation and Selection: Evaluate the generated design proposals and select the optimal design.

2 – Idea2

Co-authorship: NYC Public Cultural Life System

"The Disappear Nearby"

Under the influence of the internet and social media, people have become more inclined to stay indoors rather than socialize outside. Additionally, the uniformity in urban design has rendered many communities indistinguishable from one another. This project aims to reintroduce individuals to the "local" world around them.

By getting images from social media within a city, the project seeks to compare the visual similarities and differences of urban interfaces across various neighborhoods. Utilizing computer vision techniques, the project explores scenes and objects that represent distinct geographical information. The ultimate goal is to encourage the creation of unique elements in each community and to involve the public actively in the design process.

Idea3

Real Estate: Flowing Credit Product

Housing Wealth Evaluation in Manhattan

This project aims to explore how the architectural forms of real estate in Manhattan are influenced by economic data and fluctuations in property prices. By linking economic data with real estate price volatility as inputs, changes in apartment layouts and the overall building form are generated within Grasshopper , ultimately presented on a UI. In other words, it treats yet-to-be-designed real estate as a commodity similar to stocks or futures, allowing people to conduct advance trades on an app.
